Space Exploration

Milestones That Changed Humanity

1957
Sputnik 1 — First Satellite
The Soviet Union launched the world's first artificial satellite, marking the dawn of the Space Age and initiating the Space Race between superpowers.
1961
Yuri Gagarin — First Human in Space
Aboard Vostok 1, Gagarin completed one orbit around Earth in 108 minutes, becoming the first human to travel to outer space.
1969
Apollo 11 — Moon Landing
Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon, with Armstrong uttering the iconic words: "One small step for man..."
1990
Hubble Space Telescope Launched
NASA's Hubble opened a new window to the cosmos, delivering breathtaking images from the depths of space and revolutionizing astronomy.
2004
Mars Rovers — Spirit & Opportunity
NASA's twin rovers landed on Mars, originally planned for 90-day missions. Opportunity lasted 14 years, transforming our understanding of the Red Planet.
2021
James Webb Space Telescope
Launched on Christmas Day, JWST is the most powerful telescope ever built, capable of observing galaxies from just 300 million years after the Big Bang.

Cosmic Phenomena

Wonders of the Deep Universe

Singularity
Black Holes
Regions where gravity is so strong that nothing — not even light — can escape. Supermassive black holes anchor the centres of most galaxies.
Stellar Nursery
Nebulae
Giant clouds of gas and dust where new stars are born. The Pillars of Creation span 4–5 light-years.
Neutron Star
Pulsars
Rapidly rotating neutron stars emitting beams of electromagnetic radiation. Some spin 700 times per second.
Space Weather
Auroras
Dazzling light displays caused when solar wind particles collide with Earth's atmosphere near the magnetic poles.
Stellar Death
Supernovae
The spectacular explosion of a massive star. For weeks it can outshine an entire galaxy, seeding space with heavy elements.
